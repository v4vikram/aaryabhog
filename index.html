Aloo
Paratha

  • Mix whole wheat flour, salt, and oil in a bowl.
  • Gradually add water while mixing the flour.
  • Knead thoroughly until the dough becomes soft and smooth.
  • Cover the dough with a damp cloth for resting.
  • Let the dough rest for 15 minutes to soften.

Aloo
Paratha

  • Mash the boiled potatoes until lump-free and smooth.
  • Add chopped green chilies and cumin seeds.
  • Mix in red chili powder, garam masala, and chaat masala.
  • Add freshly chopped coriander leaves for flavor.
  • Combine everything thoroughly to create the spiced potato mixture.

Aloo
Paratha

  • Divide the rested dough into small, equal-sized balls.
  • Roll each ball into a small round circle.
  • Place 2-3 tablespoons of the potato filling in the center.
  • Gather the edges and seal tightly around the filling.
  • Roll the stuffed dough ball into a flat paratha.

Aloo
Paratha

  • Heat a tawa (griddle) until moderately hot.
  • Place the rolled paratha on the tawa and cook.
  • Flip when bubbles appear and apply ghee or butter.
  • Flip again, adding ghee, cooking both sides until golden.
  • Cook until crispy and fully cooked through.

Aloo
Paratha

  • Remove the hot paratha from the tawa once done.
  • Optionally brush with more butter or ghee.
  • Serve immediately for the best taste.
  • Pair with cooling yogurt, tangy chutney, or spicy pickle.
  • Enjoy your delicious, fresh Aloo Paratha!

Pizza

  • Combine flour, yeast, sugar, and salt in a bowl.
  • Gradually add warm water and olive oil.
  • Mix and knead until a smooth, elastic dough forms.
  • Cover the dough and let it rise in a warm place for 1-1.5 hours.
  • Punch down the dough and divide it into portions.

Pizza

  • Heat olive oil in a pan, sauté garlic until fragrant.
  • Add crushed tomatoes, salt, pepper, and Italian herbs.
  • Simmer the sauce for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  • Add a pinch of sugar to balance acidity if needed.
  • Let the sauce cool before using.

Pizza

  • Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C).
  • Roll out the dough to your desired thickness on a floured surface.
  • Spread a thin layer of sauce on the dough.
  • Add your choice of toppings: mozzarella, pepperoni, veggies, etc.
  • Sprinkle with fresh basil and drizzle olive oil on top.

Pizza

  • Transfer the pizza to a preheated pizza stone or baking tray.
  • Bake in the oven for 10-12 minutes or until the crust is golden.
  • Ensure the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  • Remove from the oven and allow it to cool slightly.
  • Cut into slices and serve hot.

Pizza

  • Garnish with extra basil, parmesan, or chili flakes if desired.
  • Serve immediately while hot.
  • Pair with a side salad or garlic bread for a complete meal.
  • Enjoy your homemade pizza with friends or family!

Dosa

  • Soak 1 cup of rice and 1/2 cup of urad dal separately for 4-6 hours.
  • Grind the soaked rice and urad dal into a smooth batter.
  • Add salt and mix thoroughly to incorporate.
  • Cover the batter and let it ferment overnight, about 8-12 hours.
  • After fermentation, the batter should be airy and slightly sour.

Dosa

  • Heat a non-stick tawa (griddle) over medium heat until hot.
  • Lightly grease the tawa with oil or butter.
  • Pour a ladle of batter in the center and spread it outwards in a circular motion.
  • Drizzle some oil or ghee around the edges to enhance crispiness.
  • Cook until the edges turn golden brown and the center is set.

Dosa

  • Flip the dosa if you prefer it fully cooked on both sides.
  • If not flipping, let it cook until it's crispy on the bottom.
  • Cook for an additional minute or two after flipping.
  • Remove the dosa from the tawa once it is evenly cooked and crispy.
  • Repeat the process for the remaining batter, adjusting the heat as needed.

Dosa

  • Sauté mustard seeds, curry leaves, and chopped onions in oil until fragrant.
  • Add boiled and mashed potatoes, mixing thoroughly with spices.
  • Season with turmeric powder, salt, and green chilies for flavor.
  • Stir well and cook for a few minutes until heated through.
  • This spiced potato mixture can be used as a filling for masala dosa.

Dosa

  • Serve the hot dosa immediately with coconut chutney and sambar.
  • For masala dosa, place the potato filling in the center and fold.
  • Pair with your favorite chutneys or pickles for added flavor.
  • Enjoy your crispy, delicious dosa fresh off the tawa!
  • Perfect for breakfast, lunch, or dinner!
