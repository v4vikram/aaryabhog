Soil Preparation: The process starts with selecting fertile soil for growing wheat. The soil is plowed and leveled to ensure good drainage and proper aeration.

Sowing Wheat Seeds: Farmers sow wheat seeds using traditional or modern machinery, usually after the monsoon or during the Rabi season (winter in India).

Irrigation: Wheat requires moderate water, so farmers irrigate the fields periodically based on the soil and weather conditions.

Fertilization and Pest Control: Organic or chemical fertilizers are added for better crop yield. Pest control measures are also taken to protect the wheat from insects and diseases.
